Intravenous Unfractionated Heparin - A comparison of methods

Introduction

Intravenous Unfractionated Heparin (IFUVH) is an anticoagulation method. It is used to treat patients who suffer from issues related to blood thickness such as blood clots or severe bleeding. The activated partial thromboplastin times (aPTTs) is the measurement used to measure a patient's performance. A patient is in therapeutic range is their aPTT value is between 63 and 95. Otherwise, a patient is considered out (Above or Below) of therapeutic range. aPTT values range from 0 to 180.

There are three methods used to calculate time a patient spents in therapeutic range. They are the Fraction of Values (FOV), Stair Step (SS) and the Linear Interpolation (LI) method. This report provides a description of each methodology and an assessment of the time spent in range based on each methodology.

The following table provides a sample of the dataset we will be working with. Each row is identified by an Episode Id, and contains the time and percent spent above, in and below therapeutic range for each episode. In total there are 4960 episodes and 7 columns. Comparison of Three Methods

The following plots exemplify the three different methodologies. The FOV method only counts the number of collections within therapeutic range and divides it by the number of collections. The SS method attributes the time between two collections to the collection value (Above, Below, Inside) of the latter collection. Then, the total time in therapeutic range is then divided by the total time.

The LI method calculates the slopes between two measurements and determines the time the aPTT crosses the lower and upper bound of the therapeutic range. Similarly, the total time inside therapeutic range is divided by the total duration to obtain the percent of time inside therapeutic range.

From the graphs above, FOV measures no time in therapeutic range but 75% above therapeutic range (3 collections above range divided by a total of 4). On the other hand, SS calculates 100% of time above therapeutic range since all the time between collections are above therapeutic range. Lastly, LI calculates some time above, inside and below therapeutic range since the line between collections spents time above, inside and below therapeutic range.

We looked at the absolute pariwsie difference of each method to determine if these differences differed significantly from each other. The boxplot below provides the distributions of the absolute difference between methods. A mean for the absolute difference is also provided below.

FunDiff<-FSL[,c("Episode_Id","SS_Total","FOV_PercentInside","SS_PercentInside","LI_PercentInside")]
FunDiff$FOV_SS<-(FunDiff$FOV_PercentInside-FunDiff$SS_PercentInside)*100
FunDiff$FOV_LI<-(FunDiff$FOV_PercentInside-FunDiff$LI_PercentInside)*100
FunDiff$SS_LI<-(FunDiff$SS_PercentInside-FunDiff$LI_PercentInside)*100

boxplot(abs(FunDiff$FOV_SS),abs(FunDiff$FOV_LI),abs(FunDiff$SS_LI),
        main="Pairwise Comparison",names=c("FOV-SS","FOV-LI","SS-LI"),ylab="Absolute Differences",ylim=c(0,100),col="steelblue")

Our mean tells us that on average, FOV and SS, FOV and LI, and SS and LI differ by about 7.3%, 9.9% and 11.1% respectively. Our boxpot shows us that differences can range anywhere from 0 % up to over 90% in the SS_LI case. A t-test was performed on all three distributions to determine if these differences were significant. The p-values for each test are reported below.

t.test(abs(FunDiff$FOV_SS))$p.value

With significant p values for all three cases, we can conclude that each methodology does differ significantly from each other.

Lastly, we hypothesized that the difference between methods is larger for patients who had a shorter treatment time. The plots below plot the difference (not absolute difference) between methods against the total treatment time.

Our graph shows us that the episodes with a shorter treatment time showed higher variation in the differences between the methods.Thus, we conclude that these methods are not compatible for calculating time within therapeutic range. We believe that LI should be adopted in favor of SS and FOV because it accounts for the time between collections, the variation of aPTT between collections and the continuous nature of aPTT.
